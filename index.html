Mac Crew — The 3 Streams

Mac Crew is more than a loyalty program — it's the community layer that keeps adult sailors connected to MacSailing year-round. Know these streams so you can talk about them confidently with graduates.

Mac Crew: Learn

For CANSail 1 Graduates

Skill-building sessions, coached sails, and social events designed for new sailors finding their feet. A supportive, low-pressure environment to keep the momentum going after CANSail 1. Community is central — these sailors are just starting their journey and need to feel connected.

CANSail 1 GradsCoached SailsSocial EventsBeginner-Friendly

Mac Crew: Race

For CANSail 2 Graduates

Intro to racing, tactics, mark roundings, and fleet sailing. Competitive but inclusive — about the experience as much as the results. Social racing nights, crew matching for those without a boat, and a tight community of sailors who want to push their skills.

CANSail 2 GradsRacing TacticsFleet SailingRacing Nights

Mac Crew: Cruise

For Basic Cruising Graduates

Keelboat passages and coastal cruising. For sailors ready to go further — day sails and longer coastal passages. A more adventurous community with shared passion for exploration and seamanship.

Basic Cruising GradsKeelboatCoastal PassagesOffshore Skills
🌊
Your role with Mac Crew: When you complete a course with adult graduates, connect them to the right stream. A warm handoff — "You should check out Mac Crew: Learn, here's what it is" — is one of the most valuable things you can do.

What You Do at Mac Crew Sessions

📚
At Learn Sessions

Facilitated coached sails — structured but relaxed. Focus on confidence and connection. Keep it social, low-pressure, and encouraging. These sailors are still finding their feet. Help them feel like they belong.

🏆
At Race Sessions

Run structured racing drills — mark roundings, starts, tactics, fleet positioning. Keep it competitive but inclusive. Debrief after every race. These sailors want to push their skills — give them specific, honest feedback.

⚓
At Cruise Sessions

Lead passage planning and coastal sailing. Cover weather assessment, tides, navigation basics, and seamanship. These sailors are building toward independence on the water — coach them to think, not just follow instructions.

Customer Service

Every interaction shapes MacSailing's reputation — and whether someone comes back.

1st
Impressions matter
∞
Patience required
0
Gossip tolerance

Standards

  • Be friendly and welcoming with students, parents, and guests at all times
  • Use inclusive, age-appropriate language with every group
  • Avoid gossip, venting, or unprofessional conversations near customers or junior staff
  • Maintain a professional appearance — uniforms on, boats tidy, gear clean
  • Phones away on the water unless for safety or navigation

Handling Upset Customers

💬
The Golden Rule: Stay calm → Listen briefly → Pass to a manager. Never argue, never over-promise, never over-explain.
1

Stay Calm

Your calm energy is contagious. Do not match their frustration level under any circumstances.

2

Acknowledge

"I hear you, that sounds frustrating." You don't need to solve it — just show you're listening.

3

Pass to a Manager

Politely connect them with the right person. Then follow up to make sure they got there.

Vancouver by Sea — Elevated Standard

🚢
Higher bar. Vancouver by Sea guests are paying for a premium experience. Introduce yourself by name, anticipate needs before they're asked, and treat the boat with the care and attention it deserves at all times.

Module 5

The Customer Journey

From first click to loyal community member — your role touches every stage of this journey.

In 2026 the goal is retention as much as acquisition. We want people to take a course, join Mac Crew, and stay connected to MacSailing for years. You're the most important factor in whether that happens.

Youth Camp Journey

1

📣 Discovery & Registration

Parent finds MacSailing online and registers. Their expectations are set by our website. They arrive expecting safe, organized, and fun.

2

👋 Arrival & Drop-Off

You greet families at the beach. Warm, organized, in uniform. This moment sets the tone for the entire week — get it right.

3

⛵ On-Water Experience

This is the product. Energy, safety, coaching, fun. Your coaching quality is what MacSailing is selling.

4

🌞 Lunch & Land Time

You're still working. Engage with students, supervise actively. These moments build the relationships that make kids beg their parents to come back.

5

🏠 Pick-Up & Parent Moment

Organized dismissal. Share one specific positive moment with each parent: "Emma landed her first tack independently today." File any incident reports before families leave.

6

🔄 Return, Growth & Community

Stoked students come back next year — and bring friends. For older youth (12–15), the new cruising stream gives them somewhere to grow into. That progression is the retention engine.

Adult Journey → Mac Crew Pipeline

  • Books a course → expects professional, structured learning
  • First session → introduce yourself, set expectations, explain the progression path
  • Mid-course → honest conversations about progress, Checklick check-in
  • Completion → certification filed, warm handoff to the right Mac Crew stream
  • Mac Crew member → now part of the community, attends socials, refers friends
  • Advanced pathway → Learn → Race or Cruise → Vancouver by Sea sails
🌟
The 2026 goal: Every adult who completes a course leaves knowing which Mac Crew stream is right for them. That warm handoff is your job — and it's the most important retention action you can take.

Scheduling, Comms & Standards

How we communicate, how shifts work, what we expect, and how you're rewarded for a great season.

Scheduling

  • Google Calendar is how shifts are posted — check it regularly and keep it visible
  • Shifts are posted at least 2 weeks in advance
  • Changes within 2 weeks will be communicated directly to you
  • Privates marked TBC means you're on call — a student may be booked. Within 48 hours of the session time, if you haven't heard from us, assume no lesson
  • If a booking comes in within 48 hours, we will reach out to you directly
📅
Can't make a shift? Post in the WhatsApp Shift Coverage group with a valid reason. Avoid last-minute unless it's a genuine emergency. Repeated last-minute cancellations affect your performance rating and end-of-season bonus.

Hours & Pay

  • Sign in at the start of your shift, sign out at the end
  • Sign out for long breaks between programs — do NOT sign out for lunch
  • Pay periods: 1–15 and 16–end of month. Paycheque within one week of period end
  • Any payroll questions go to Charlie Boettcher

Progressive Discipline System

New in 2026. This system ensures fairness, accountability, and clear expectations for everyone.

1st Write-Up — Verbal Warning

  • Conversation with supervisor — issue documented internally
  • Staff member loses 50% of bonus eligibility

2nd Write-Up — Written Warning

  • Formal written documentation with clear expectations for improvement
  • Staff member loses 100% of bonus eligibility

3rd Write-Up — Dismissal

  • Termination of employment

What Can Lead to a Write-Up

⏰
Attendance & Reliability
  • Repeatedly late to shifts or classes
  • No-shows or last-minute cancellations without valid reason
  • Leaving early without approval
📋
Failure to Follow Procedures
  • Not completing required checklists or logs
  • Ignoring radio protocols
  • Not following lesson plans or program structure without valid reason
🔧
Equipment & Property
  • Negligent damage to boats or equipment
  • Not reporting damage or issues
👔
Professional Conduct
  • Inappropriate language or behaviour with participants or staff
  • Poor attitude, lack of teamwork, or refusal to follow direction
  • Not representing MacSailing professionally
  • Phone use during program time that distracts from supervision or safety
🛡️
Safety Violations
  • Not enforcing or following lifejacket rules
  • Unsafe boat operation or poor judgment on the water
  • Ignoring weather limits or instructions from senior staff
  • Not wearing a kill cord while operating a coach boat
  • Improper use of a coach boat or unsafe positioning around students
  • Smoking, vaping, alcohol, or drug use while at work
💡
Most issues are preventable with good communication and effort. If you're unsure about anything — ask before acting. That is always the right move.
🚫
Phone policy: Phones are not permitted on the water during sessions except for safety or navigation. Taking photos of students on the water is permitted only when explicitly approved by a manager — and only by the person given that permission. Phone use in front of students or guests for any other purpose is unprofessional and affects your end-of-season bonus.

End-of-Season Bonus

🎉
MacSailing rewards a great season. An end-of-season bonus is available to all staff, with the amount based on your role. Your performance across the season determines whether you receive it in full.
✅
What earns your full bonus
  • Consistent punctuality and reliability throughout the season
  • Safety protocols followed without exception
  • Logs, Checklick, and admin completed on time
  • Professional conduct — uniform, phone policy, attitude
  • Positive contribution to team culture and junior staff development
  • Strong student and guest experience delivery
⚠️
What affects your bonus
  • Repeated late arrivals or last-minute shift cancellations
  • Phone use in front of students, parents, or guests
  • Incomplete logs, missed Checklick uploads, or unreported repairs
  • Safety protocol failures
  • Unprofessional conduct or conduct inconsistent with MacSailing values
💬
Bonus amounts vary by role and will be communicated to you directly. If you have questions about your specific bonus structure, speak with Alissa or Nick.
🔧
Equipment: Things break — report it, don't ignore it.
Pink tape on a boat or piece of equipment means it is out of service — do not use it under any circumstances.
Log all repairs using the Repairs Reporting Form in the Staff Hub. Check your equipment each morning. Never launch with gear you know is compromised.

CANSail Logs & Checklick

  • Run a debrief after every CANSail session — even if no log is needed
  • Logs let another instructor pick up the class if someone is sick
  • For adults who don't pass: communicate to Lorna and log it immediately
  • Upload Checklick: Thursdays end-of-day, or second-weekend Saturdays for adult classes
  • Have the progress conversation before uploading — their level should never be a surprise
  • CANSail 2.0: "developing / performing / extending" — only performing checked to pass. Extending is bonus
